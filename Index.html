Cosmos development

Deconstruct your brand universe into elemental forces.

Instead of a single “brand essence”, we work with gravitational pulls: what keeps people in your orbit, what repels them, and what creates tension.

Core components we’ll map
  • ConstellationsKey ideas, stories, and rituals that repeat across your touchpoints.
  • GravitiesPsychological pulls that explain why people stay in your ecosystem.
  • OrbitsHow different audiences circulate through your brand: newcomers, regulars, evangelists.
  • Void zonesWhere you deliberately choose not to play – the anti-positioning that sharpens your POV.

What you leave with

A practical map of your brand cosmos that turns fuzzy “vibes” into a visual system: arcs, tensions, and key worlds for storytelling. Something your strategy, content, and design teams can all read together.

Story engine

Questions that keep your cosmos generating stories.

These prompts are not campaign ideas. They’re reusable questions that teams can return to when they’re stuck, overwhelmed, or sliding back into generic territory.

Origin

If your brand was a world that suddenly appeared, what problem in the “old universe” did it quietly rebel against?

Characters

Who thrives in your cosmos that struggles in others? What small, specific behaviour signals “they belong here”?

Rituals

What repeated action — daily, weekly, seasonal — could anchor people deeper into your world without feeling like “engagement hacks”?

Conflict & tension

Every strong cosmos has something worth fighting for.

We locate the productive friction in your world — the conflict that makes your brand feel alive, not just “nice”.

  • Frictions Where your audience’s reality clashes with the dominant narrative in your category.
  • Antagonists Not competitors, but ideas, habits, or systems your brand gently or boldly opposes.
  • Allies The people, tools, or communities that help your customers move through the conflict.

Output

A short conflict statement plus three story lanes you can immediately plug into campaigns, onboarding, and product storytelling.
